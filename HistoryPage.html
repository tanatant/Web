Dinagyang Festival

Origins

Origin (1967–1968): The celebration began after Rev. Fr. Ambrosio Galindez of the San Jose Parish introduced devotion to the Santo Niño. In 1968, a replica of the image was brought to Iloilo from Cebu, prompting a grand reception.

Ati-atihan Roots

Originally known as "Iloilo Ati-Atihan," the festival was patterned after the Ati-Atihan of Ibajay, Aklan.

Cultural Significance

The festival honors the legendary barter of Panay Island, where Malay settlers purchased the island from the Ati people.

Evolution

It evolved from a small parish celebration into a large-scale cultural event featuring the famous Ati Tribe competition with dancers in vibrant costumes and rhythmic drum performances.

Naming

The term "Dinagyang" was coined to describe the lively, joyful, and energetic nature of the celebration.
