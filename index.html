Dinagyang Festival

The Dinagyang Festival is a major religious and cultural celebration held every fourth Sunday of January in Iloilo City, Philippines. It honors the Santo Niño (Holy Child Jesus) and commemorates the historic pact between the Malay settlers and the indigenous Ati people of Panay.

Known as one of the country’s largest festivals, it draws up to two million visitors each year. The event is famous for its Ati Tribes Competition featuring vibrant warrior costumes, rhythmic drum performances, and choreographed street dancing, along with highlights such as the Kasadyahan Festival, ILOmination night parade, and solemn religious processions.
